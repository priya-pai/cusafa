EcoFeminism

Why is environmental justice often isolated from other forms of social justice? Why is it simple to remove environmental concerns from social ones? How has the environmental justice movement been historically white and privledged? And what does it look like to approach feminism from an eco-centric lens? SAFA is excited to bring Karen Washington and Aditi Varshneya to Columbia to engage in a conversation about ecofeminism, the intersection between feminism and environmental justice in New York City.

FGM: A Human Rights Issue?

The panelists above will discuss the integration of female genital mutilation into broader human rights discourse and implications of the Detroit case on both US and global advocacy movements to end FGM. A screening of survivor testimonies will precede the discussion, which will be followed by ways to take action.

South Asian Gendered Violence Conference

What does gendered violence mean in the context of religion, culture, and socioeconomic status in South Asian countries? Why do these countries continue to target their weakest and most oppressed? How can we fight for liberation? Join us for a weekend of exploring South Asian gender justice and feminism through musical performances, a short film screening, and discussions.

(Dis)Orientation

CU SAFA, in collaboration with AAA (Asian American Alliance), Mujeres and NAC (Native American Council), is extremely excited to present our (Dis)Orientation event. Join us in a conversation on how to begin successfully navigating this university, taking into account identity, power & socioeconomic dynamics, and social activism on campus. This event is catered for first years but all are welcome as being an active member of this campus is a constant process of learning and unlearning. We hope to see you there!

Critical Perspectives on Migration

Join CU South Asian Feminism(s) Alliance, UndoCU, and Asian American Alliance for a panel discussion focused on questions of historical migration and colonialism, resources for immigrant or undocumented students, and awareness of the changes to DACA and immigration in the Trump era. The event will be a conversation about immigration and undocumented communities in the current political climate, along with discussing sustainable forms of action and solidarity networks for immigrant communities.

The Price of Silence

SAFA and No Red Tape present "The Price of Silence: Sexual Violence in the South Asian Context" in hopes of deconstructing the stigma around sexual violence within South Asia and the diaspora. A 12-minute film, followed by a discussion.

Islamophobia in South Asia & the Diaspora

This is an informative panel and teach-in led by professors at Barnard and Columbia. We acknowledge the alliance of Hindu nationalism with Trump and the rise in hate crimes in both South Asia and in America against Muslim, Dalit, and Adivasi communities. While much organizing has been done against Islamophobic sentiments perpetuated by non-South Asian and non-Middle Eastern groups, there is very little critical thought about Islamophobia within our communities. We hope to deconstruct the colonialist history and origins of anti-Muslim hatred within South Asia and the Diaspora.
